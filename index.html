Question #1 : We want to hear about your education & professional career to date. Tell us about your tech journey or why you want to start your tech journey now. Please include experiences that have included coding or data science or that have led you to want to become a software engineer or data scientist.

I started my professional career working as a budget analyst in Washington DC after I received my bachelor’s degree in Economics from the University of Virginia. Since this job was in a university, I worked alongside several professors who were truly passionate about teaching. I was so inspired by them that I started reading about the philosophy of education, which led me to Paulo Freire’s book “Pedagogy of the Oppressed.” This book was so convincing that it drastically changed my outlook on life and inspired me to become a teacher, which is my current profession.

It wasn’t until the COVID lockdown, during which I had ample free time, that I dabbled in programming. I remember the excitement I felt from challenging myself to solve problems and think in different ways. For the first time since college it felt like I was growing intellectually, thereby becoming a better version of myself. The satisfaction that I felt in myself from learning this new skill is what ultimately convinced me to change careers.
Since beginning this journey, I have taught myself concepts such as form creation and validation in HTML, flexbox and the grid layout in CSS and higher order functions, closures and recursion in Javascript. I have also started studying algorithms and data structures in order to write code more efficiently.

While I am proud of coming this far, I know that I have a lot more to learn, which is why I want to catapult my learning with Hack Reactor.
Question #2 : Tell us about your proudest accomplishment so far, in any aspect of your life.

Without a doubt my greatest accomplishment has been the personal guidance I’ve given to my students. Upon becoming a teacher, I had the mission of enhancing my students’ ability to think independently and critically. While this has continued to be a goal of mine throughout my career as an educator, I quickly realized that it only forms a fraction of a teacher’s “moral curriculum.” Other important qualities such as compassion, work ethic, tolerance, perseverance, self-expression, etc. have become pillars in my educational approach. The teaching of these qualities has taken form in several ways: from me leading discussions with students on International Women’s Day about the historical and present-day struggles of women, to me wearing a fairy costume on Halloween, which resulted in me unexpectedly coming out to my students, who were puzzled to see a man dressed as such.

My mission as a teacher changed. What started as a mission to almost exclusively teach critical thinking skills turned into me attempting to become an exemplar of morality for them. This transformation into a role model caused me to scrutinize my own behaviors so that they would be in accordance with the expectations that I placed on my students. Because of this, teaching core values is my ultimate accomplishment to date, not only because it induces desirable behavioral changes in my students, but also because it forces me to look at my own actions to ensure that I am personally living up to the standards I set for my students.

Question #3 : Please describe why you would benefit from the scholarship and what you would plan to do with your new skills.
Although I have found great satisfaction in my career as an educator, the low pay rate has left me in a position where I am living paycheck to paycheck without any savings and with debts that equate to half of my annual income. Given these financial constraints, I find myself quite limited in how I can navigate the future, both during and after the bootcamp. I believe that with the scholarship I would be able to make the most out of my time at the bootcamp, since it would provide me with the financial freedom to take out a personal loan to create a more stable living situation.
